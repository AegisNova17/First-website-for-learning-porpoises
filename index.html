For Learning Porpoises

Dolphins communicate using a variety of whistles, clicks, and pulsed sounds, along with body language. They use these sounds to socialize, navigate, and hunt. Bottlenose dolphins, for example, have unique "signature whistles" that may function like names, allowing them to identify each other. While researchers haven't discovered a complex language in dolphins like human languages, they are actively studying dolphin communication to better understand their complex vocalizations
